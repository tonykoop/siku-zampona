# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

A single completed Andean siku (zampoña) panpipe — the Zanka tenor rank — photographed as a real physical object on a clean neutral light-grey studio sweep. The instrument is a flat raft of thirteen vertical cylindrical tubes bound side by side, descending in a stepped staircase from the longest tube at the left to the shortest at the right; all tube tops are flush along a single straight blow-edge while the lower closed ends fall away in a smooth diagonal step profile, the silhouette that makes a siku instantly recognizable. The tubes read as warm honey-toned bamboo cane with faint natural nodes and a soft satin tung-oil sheen, roughly thumb-width in diameter. They are sandwiched between two slender hardwood frame rails of oiled cherry running horizontally across the array, and lashed with bands of waxed linen cord wrapped in neat parallel turns near each rail. Realistic hand-held scale — the raft is about as wide as two spread hands. Soft directional studio light from upper left, gentle falloff, 3/4 product-photography angle, ~50mm lens, shallow but controlled depth of field, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## In-Context Prompt

The same Zanka siku resting on a workshop bench beside a coil of waxed linen cord, a small block of beeswax, and a digital caliper, lit by soft north-facing window light. Honey bamboo tubes and oiled cherry rails catch a warm daylight glow; pale wood shavings and a story stick lie blurred in the background. Environmental maker-bench storytelling, natural light, shallow depth of field, the stepped tube array in crisp focus. Concept visualization only.

## Detail Prompt

Macro close-up raking across the blow-edge of the siku: the top openings of several bamboo tubes seen in a row, each a clean dark circular bore mouth rimmed by the pale cane wall, flush along one straight edge. Hard low side-light skims the surface to reveal cane grain, the faint ring of a natural node, the satin oil finish, and the tightly wrapped waxed-cord lashing binding the tubes to the cherry rail just below. Extreme texture and depth, high detail, no measurements.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, or fake maker marks; no dimension lines or measurement callouts; no curved/conical/recorder-style bodies or finger holes (siku tubes are straight cylinders with no side holes); no impossible mechanisms; anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
